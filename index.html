Each concert season brings live music of all kinds to be enjoyed by all who live in or are just visiting our beautiful mountain community. Bring your family and friends to experience the exciting sounds of the Oakhurst Community Concert Band!

Our next concert will be Sunday, November 19, 2017, 3:00 PM, at Mountain Christian Center  with this repertoire for the Concert: (TBD) You do not want to miss this one, it will be fun and packed with energy and excitement!

About

 

 

The Oakhurst Community Band, as we know it now, is the result of a few musician's lifelong love of music and his refusal to "let the music die". Loren Callan, and his wife Betty, retired to Oakhurst in 1998 and Loren began playing his trumpet with the Oakhurst Community Band. When then band director, Michael Corrigan (who initially started the band) became too busy to give the band the attention he wanted to give it, Loren was happy to take over directing duties, drawing on his experience as a musician and director during his school years. After Loren's death in December, 2003, Randy Hyatt, band director at Yosemite High School and Loren's assistant director, took over as director of the band. In late 2016, Randy Hyatt retired and a re-energized Michael Corrigan once more took over the helm as director.
_____________________________________________________________

Concerts are traditionally performed three times each year with rehearsals held in Yosemite High School's band room. The band members are all volunteer and range in age from Junior High School students to octogenarians. Many of the band members volunteer time to mentor school students throughout the mountain communities and also play with the high school band for home football games and graduation ceremonies. Those interested in seeing a band-member list can reference the latest program hand-out. For archived lists visit our Past Seasons area here.
_____________________________________________________________

Becoming The Oakhurst Community Concert Band, Inc., a 501(c)(3) non-profit organization in 2002, the band is supported by generous donations from corporate sponsors and community service groups, as well as individual music lovers. A Board of Directors made up of band members meets throughout the year to make decisions regarding expenditures and concert arrangements.

Board of Directors / Staff

Director
- Mike Corrigan                                           (photo by Steve Montalto)


Mike has been an instrumental music educator in California for forty-five years. he received his bachelor’s degree in music from California State University, Long Beach. He served for several seasons as conductor of the Pacific Symphony Youth Wind Ensemble, an auditioned group which drew students from Los Angeles, Orange, Riverside, San Bernardino, and San Diego Counties. They performed in the new Renee and Henry Segerstrom Concert Hall of the Orange County Performing Arts Center.

Mike has previously taught at Mater Dei High School (twenty-five years), Yosemite High School (five years), Clovis High School (seven years), and Aliso Niguel High School (two years). Mike taught as band director at Santa Monica High School (three years), and in Manhattan Beach Unified School District (two years). He also directed the Santa Monica College Emeritus Concert Band for 5 years. He has produced highly successful marching bands at each of his assignments (including recent state champions at Aliso Niguel). His groups have consistently received unanimous superior ratings in local festivals, as well as festivals throughout the United States, Canada, and Europe. He also served as the Music Resource Specialist for the Capistrano Unified School District during his tenure at Aliso Niguel.

Mike has served as president of the Southern California School Band and Orchestra Association (SCSBOA), and president of the California Band Directors Association (CBDA). He served as a Head Judge for SCSBOA. He has adjudicated numerous festival and marching band events throughout the United States, and is frequently called to guest conduct select honor groups. His bands and orchestras have received “Unanimous Gold” recognitions at Carnegie Hall and The Kennedy Center.

His bands and orchestras have traveled extensively. Past performances include:
Three European Tours (Germany, Austria, Switzerland, Italy, England, Ireland).
Two Canadian Tours (Vancouver, Calgary)
Two Western United States Tours (California Oregon, Washington, Idaho, Nevada).
Two Hawaiian Islands Tours (Oahu, Maui, Hawaii, Molokai).
Two New York Tours (Carnegie Hall).
One Washington D.C. Tour (Kennedy Center).

And add to that, The Walt Disney Concert Hall, as well!

Mike has received recognition from many music educator organizations, including:
Hall of Fame award from the California Music Educators Association (2017).
Legion of Honor Award - John Philip Sousa National Foundation.
Distinguished Service Award - California Band Directors Association.
Honorary Life Member - Southern California School Band and Orchestra Assoc.

 Retiring Conductor
 - Randy Hyatt                                              (photo by Steve Montalto)


Randy Hyatt became Conductor of the Oakhurst Community Concert Band in November, 2003, succeeding the late Loren Callan. He retired in Dec 2016 and handed the baton over to band-founder Michael Corrigan. Randy has been active in music and music education for over 27 years, including a former stint as band and choir instructor at Yosemite High School. He received his BA in Music Education from CSU Fresno, and also has an MA in Education Administration. Throughout his teaching career Randy has held various positions providing experience in every area of music education. While teaching at Roosevelt School of the Arts in Fresno he worked with several students who have continued on into prestigious music careers, including Tony Award winner Audra McDonald. Outside of school, Randy has been active in community events including staging productions at Saroyan Theatre for Northwest Church. As Conductor of The Oakhurst Community Concert Band, Randy worked enthusiastically to continue Loren's dream of a first class community band providing memorable concerts and a venue for musicians of all ages to come together to share the joy of playing music.

 Assistant Conductor
- Pat Mierkey                                               (photo by Steve Montalto)



Pat has a lifetime of music experience teaching and playing throughout the United States. Pat began his professional musical career at the ripe age of fourteen by playing trumpet and saxophone with a polka/dance band in central Wisconsin. He became a music teacher in 1965 after earning a degree in music education in Arizona. He played euphonium, trumpet, and tuba with the Arizona 108th Army National Guard Band for thirteen years. He began with the Yosemite Jazz Band on tuba in 1996 and is now playing his trombone.

 Assistant Director
- Bill Samuelson                                            (photo by Steve Montalto)

Bill is a frequent contributing conductor for the OCCB, and is highly respected for ability to direct jazz and rock ensembles of every ilk. He recently retired as Minarets High School's music director.
